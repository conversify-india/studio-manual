Trim → Transcribe Workflow Guide

Welcome! This guide will teach you how to convert video and audio files into text using our translation tool. We will show you how to trim your files first, and then upload them to get word-by-word reports.

How it Works

First, we use a tool on our computer called Video Trimmer to cut the audio or video file so it only includes the part we need. Then, we upload this smaller file to the LingoChaps Transcription Tool. This tool will transcribe the audio, translate it to English, and create a Word document report for us.

About the Video Trimmer & LingoChaps Transcription Tool

We use two main tools in this workflow: the Video Trimmer (to cut the files) and the LingoChaps Transcription Tool (to write down and translate the audio).

What does the LingoChaps Transcription Tool do?

The LingoChaps Transcription Tool uses AI to listen to video or audio files, write down what people are saying, translate it into English, and check for any text shown on the screen.

Why must we trim files before uploading to the LingoChaps Transcription Tool?

The LingoChaps Transcription Tool is used after we have trimmed the files. We must trim files because:

  • Smaller files upload much faster.
  • The AI processes shorter files much quicker.
  • It saves cost and keeps the final reports clean.
Rule: Only upload trimmed files to the LingoChaps Transcription Tool!

Stage 01: Video Trimmer (Local Preparation)

Start by trimming your video or audio file on your computer using the Video Trimmer tool.

1

Open the source file

Click the Open Video button and select your video or audio file from your computer. If you have an audio-only file, turn on the Video / Audio only switch at the top.

Formats: MP4, MKV, MOV, MP3, M4A, AMR, WAV, etc.
2

Mark the range you need

Drag the slider bar to find where your clip starts and ends. Click Set Start and Set End. Then click Add to List to save this clip. You can do this multiple times if you need to cut out more than one clip.

3

Or use Auto-split instead

If you want to cut the file into equal parts, use the Auto-split box. Type the number of parts you want in Into ___ parts and click Generate. Or, set a specific time limit in Every ___ h ___ m ___ s and click Generate. This cuts a long recording into small clips very quickly.

  • If you split a 30-minute file Into 2 parts, you get:
    • Part 1: 00:00:00 to 00:15:00
    • Part 2: 00:15:00 to 00:30:00
  • Saved files will have a [trimmed] suffix at the end of their name.
  • The status bar at the bottom will say "All segments trimmed!" when done.
4

Pick a save folder

Click Choose... next to Save folder to choose where you want to save your clips. If you do not choose one, it will save them to a folder called trimmed video inside your Downloads folder.

5

Convert to MP3 if you only need audio

Check the Convert to MP3 box before exporting if you only need the sound and not the video. This makes the file size much smaller.

Recommended for call recordings like AMR/MP3 files
6

Export

Click the Trim All button to start cutting the clips. When finished, click Download or Open Folder to find your trimmed files. You will upload these files in Stage 02.

Stage 02: Upload & Transcribe

Once you have trimmed your file, open the LingoChaps Transcription Tool (studio.lingochaps.com). Since this is an internal tool, you must sign in with your official @lingochaps email.

1

Sign in & open Transcription

Log in with your official Lingochaps ID. Click the Transcription card on the left menu to open a new job.

2

Step 1 — Upload the trimmed file

Click the upload box or drag your trimmed file into it. The system accepts both video files (like MP4) and audio files (like MP3).

3

Step 2 — Set languages and hints

Keep the Languages field as auto-detect if you want the tool to find the language automatically. If you know the language, type it in (for example: "fa, en"). You can also write special requests in the Custom instructions box (optional).

4

Choose your output mode

Choose the option that fits what the client asked for:

  • Full — transcription + on-screen text: Writes down all speech, translates it to English, and finds any text shown on the video screen (using OCR screen snapshots).
  • Transcript only: Writes down speech and translates it. It does not look at the video screen. (This option is faster).
  • On-screen text only: Only finds and translates text shown on the video screen. It does not write down the spoken words.

Back-up server trimming

You can check Process only part of the video to set start/end times inside the LingoChaps Transcription Tool. But you should only use this as a backup. Trimming files locally on your computer first is always the main, preferred way.

5

Step 3 — Start transcription

Click the purple Start transcription button. This button will light up only after you have uploaded your file.

6

Track your job in Recent jobs

Your job will show up in the Recent jobs list below. You can watch the status badge and progress bar update.

  • DONE / 100%: The job is finished and ready for review.
  • ERROR: The upload failed. Please trim the file again and upload it.
  • Refresh: Click the refresh button to see the latest progress.
  • Delete: Click the trash bin icon to delete the job from history. This will delete the files permanently.

Stage 03: Review & Download Outputs

Open your finished job to check the text and download the Word report.

1

Open the job details

Click on any job that says DONE. A side panel will open to show you the details.

2

Download the Word report

Click the Download Word report button to save the file to your computer. The report contains a table with the spoken words, English translations, and notes. If you selected the 'Full' option, it will also include video screenshots.

3

Read the text on your screen

You can read the transcription on the page. Click on any minute folder (like "Minute 1" or "Minute 2") to expand and show the text segments inside.

4

Understand the segments

Each section of text shows the speaker's name, their language, and the timestamp. The original spoken text is on the left, and the English translation is on the right. Special local words or proper nouns will have small explanation notes below them.

  • Format: Female Speaker 1 [Hindi] 00:07:09 to 00:07:15
  • Transcription (Left): The words written down in their original language.
  • Translation (Right): The English translation of those words.
  • Explanatory note: A note explaining any special names, cultural words, or slang.
