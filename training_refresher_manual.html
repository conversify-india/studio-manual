1. Split Screen Configuration

How to position your desktop workspace so that reference videos/audios and translation documents are visible concurrently.

Source Window (50%)
Video / Audio / Reference
Google Doc (50%)
Translation Template

Setup Guide:

To avoid clicking back and forth between browser tabs and losing context, split your monitor equally:

On Windows Systems:

  1. Select the browser tab with the video or audio playback.
  2. Press and hold the Win key, then press the ← (Left Arrow) key. The window will snap to the left 50% of the screen.
  3. A selection panel of other active windows will display on the right. Select your Google Doc.

On macOS Systems:

  1. Hover your mouse pointer over the green full-screen icon in the upper-left corner of the source window.
  2. From the dropdown menu, click Tile Window to Left of Screen.
  3. Click the window containing your Google Doc to tile it on the right side.
Pro Tip: If you are working on a smaller laptop monitor, adjust the divider line slightly to give 60% of the screen to the Google Doc and 40% to the video playback.

2. Copy-Pasting & Spacing Rules

Best practices for transferring transcripts into Google Docs without breaking template formats and keeping text neatly spaced.

A. Pasting Plain Text

When you copy transcription segments from LingoChaps Studio or online text fields, they carry over background styles. Standard pasting will alter the target template's fonts, colors, and line spacing.

The Golden Rule: Always paste plain text without formatting using these specific commands:
Windows: Ctrl + Shift + V
macOS: Cmd + Shift + V

B. Handling Paragraph & Line Spacing

  • No Empty Line Carriage Returns: Avoid hitting Enter twice to create vertical whitespace. Instead, select the text, go to the top menu, choose Format > Line & paragraph spacing, and click Add space after paragraph.
  • Speaker Break Spacing: Separate different speaker turns and new timestamps with exactly one blank paragraph line. Inside a speaker's turn, keep sentences contiguous.

3. Download & Uploading Procedure

Standard steps to save raw files locally, verify sizes, and upload them back to designated Google Drive folders.

A. Local Download Pipeline

  1. Never download files onto your Desktop. This clutter causes file mix-ups.
  2. Create a folder on your system local disk: D:\Videostowords\.
  3. Save all downloaded MP4/MP3 files into this folder.
  4. Confirm completeness: Ensure that the download progress bar has fully finished. Check the file size on your disk against the server size to prevent working on a corrupted, half-downloaded file.

B. Cloud Upload Pipeline

  1. Open the LingoChaps shared Google Drive.
  2. Navigate to the folder corresponding to the current date and client code.
  3. Drag the file from your local disk D:\Videostowords\ into the folder.
  4. Once uploaded, right-click the file in Drive, select Share > Copy Link. Make sure it is shareable, and paste the link into the Allocation Sheet.

5. ABBYY FineReader OCR Reading

How to use ABBYY FineReader to convert image-based PDFs or screenshots into editable text for translation check.

What is ABBYY FineReader?

It is an Optical Character Recognition (OCR) software that converts scanned documents, PDFs, and images containing text (such as foreign dialogue cards or video subtitles) into digital, selectable, and editable layouts.

Standard Operating Procedure:

  1. Launch **ABBYY FineReader** on your workstation.
  2. Select **Open PDF/Image Document** and choose your target file.
  3. Set the document language (e.g. Arabic, Persian, English) in the toolbar.
  4. Click the **Recognize** button to scan the layout.
  5. Verification Phase (Critical): ABBYY will highlight words with low-confidence recognition in blue or red. Review these specific areas side-by-side against the original image. Correct character reading errors manually.
  6. Highlight the verified text blocks, click copy, and paste them into your Google Doc using Ctrl+Shift+V / Cmd+Shift+V.

6. Audio Listening, Video Playback & Google Doc Comments

How to transcribe complex audio segments and record questions using standardized comment tags in Google Docs.

A. Precise Audio Listening Rules

  • Wear Headphones: Over-ear headphones are required. Do not use open speakers.
  • Adjust Playback Speed: When speech is fast, dialect-heavy, or overlaps, slow down playback in your media player to **0.75x** or **0.8x**.
  • Three-Times Rule: Play unclear audio segments at least **three times** at different volumes and speeds before flagging.

B. Adding Context Comments in Google Docs

When spelling, proper nouns, or audio words remain unclear, insert a comment using Ctrl + Alt + M (Windows) or Cmd + Option + M (Mac) and apply these exact tags:

Standard Tag When to Use Example Comment Form
[UNCLEAR @ MM:SS] Muffled speech, background noises, overlapping dialogues. [UNCLEAR @ 04:12] Sounds like "distribute" or "attribute".
[TERM QUERY] Specific technical terminology needing client check. [TERM QUERY] Local industry reference for logistics framework.
[PROPER NOUN] Names, locations, or brands whose spellings are unverified. [PROPER NOUN] Spelled phonetic name "Snigdha" - check.
